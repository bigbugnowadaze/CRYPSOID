Existing soft shapes worth raiding, but not simply copying
There are known non-Gaussian families, but none of them are enough by themselves.

Generalized normal / exponential-power distributions replace the Gaussian’s squared exponent with a shape parameter, so they can become pointier or flatter, but they are still basically center-distance distributions. SciPy describes the generalized normal as an exponential-power distribution with a single shape parameter β, and R’s gnorm documentation gives the density as an exponential of |x-μ|^β. 

q-Gaussians are more interesting because they can have heavy tails for some q values and compact support for q < 1, while recovering the ordinary Gaussian as q → 1. But they are still “Gaussian-family alternatives,” not a new scene-geometry primitive. 

Wendland / compactly supported radial basis functions matter because they show how to build smooth finite-support kernels, which is very useful for rendering efficiency and sparse interpolation. But they are still radial basis functions unless mutated. 

Matérn kernels are useful because they replace the Gaussian’s infinite smoothness with a tunable roughness/smoothness parameter, and the Gaussian/RBF appears as a limiting case as smoothness goes to infinity. That is useful for texture/material roughness, but still not the phoxoidal base shape. 

The most important source is catastrophe optics: Airy functions describe fold caustics, Pearcey functions describe cusp caustics, and optical caustics naturally create non-bell-like lobes, folds, cusps, tails, and abrupt intensity boundaries. Pearcey’s integral is specifically used for cusp caustics, where one side of the caustic has multiple rays and the other side has fewer rays.

Short Thesis: Phoxoidal Blobs for CRYPSOID
A Gaussian splat is a renderable soft particle whose shape is produced by a quadratic exponent. Because that exponent is ellipsoidal, the natural geometry of a Gaussian splat is a fuzzy ellipsoid. Millions of these ellipsoids can reconstruct realistic scenes, but the primitive itself does not know whether it represents a curved surface, a fold, an occlusion edge, a thin sheet, a cusp, a material boundary, or a view-dependent event. It only knows center, covariance, opacity, and appearance.

A phoxoidal blob is proposed as the CRYPSOID replacement primitive for Gaussian splats.

The central claim is:

Gaussian splat : ellipsoidal quadratic exponent :: phoxoidal blob : phoxponential caustic-chart exponent.

A phoxoidal blob is not merely a Gaussian with extra metadata. It replaces the Gaussian’s fixed ellipsoidal distance field with a local generated action field. Instead of asking, “How far is this point from my center?” the phoxoidal blob asks, “What local surface, fold, cusp, boundary, or visibility event generated this point?”

In its simplest form, a phoxoidal blob uses local coordinates:

u = (s, t, n)
where s and t are tangent-chart coordinates and n is normal/depth displacement. Instead of assuming a flat local tangent plane, the blob fits a small generative surface/event germ:

Hθ(s,t)
The phoxoidal density is then:

ρ(u) = α exp(-Aθ(u))
where the action Aθ measures distance from the generated local germ, not merely distance from the blob center.

A minimal phoxoidal action is:

Aθ(u) =
(s / σs)² +
(t / σt)² +
((n - Hθ(s,t)) / σn)²
The germ Hθ(s,t) may include curvature, saddle, fold, cusp, twist, and lens terms. This allows the primitive’s natural shapes to become soft sheets, folds, cusps, ribbons, saddles, flakes, lenses, and occlusion tongues rather than only ellipsoids.

The Gaussian splat is recovered as the degenerate case where the germ collapses back into a flat or quadratic distance field. Therefore, the phoxoidal blob should be treated as a strict generalization of the Gaussian splat, not a renamed version of it.

For CRYPSOID, the pipeline becomes:

photo / video / Gaussian splat / PLY evidence
    ↓
Crypsara spots
    ↓
phoxoidal blobs
    ↓
phoxoidal scene graph
    ↓
renderable CRYPSOID scene
A Crypsara spot is a candidate local mark. A phoxoidal blob is a committed renderable scene atom.

The research question is not whether a phoxoid can slightly improve a Bunny point-cloud benchmark. Bunny is only a smoke test. The real question is:

How many Gaussian splats can one phoxoidal blob replace at equal visual or geometric error?

The hypothesis is that phoxoidal blobs should outperform Gaussian splats most strongly where scenes contain non-ellipsoidal structure: curved sheets, thin surfaces, folds, cusps, sharp silhouettes, occlusion boundaries, and material discontinuities.

The correct development system is therefore PhoxBench: an automated benchmark harness that tests Gaussian, surfel, and phoxoidal candidate primitives across synthetic truth scenes, canonical meshes, trained Gaussian-splat scenes, and real reconstruction datasets. Every run should output metrics, PNG previews, side-by-side comparisons, error heatmaps, and Pareto rankings.

The thesis of CRYPSOID’s phoxoidal absorption is simple:

A scene should not be reconstructed from millions of soft ellipsoids alone. It should be reconstructed from soft local generative events. Phoxoidal blobs are the proposed primitive for those events.
